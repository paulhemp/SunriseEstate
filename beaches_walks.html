Beaches & Walks

Explore the natural beauty of the Byron Hinterland and its pristine coastline.

The Byron Hinterland and its surrounding coastline offer an abundance of natural beauty, from pristine beaches to lush rainforest walks and spectacular waterfalls. Whether you're seeking adventure, tranquility, or a bit of both, you'll find something to inspire and rejuvenate you.

Beaches

Byron Bay Beaches

Main Beach

The central hub of Byron Bay, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and people-watching. Easily accessible with plenty of facilities and a vibrant atmosphere.

Clarkes Beach

A popular spot for families and beginner surfers, offering calmer waters than Main Beach and beautiful views of the iconic Byron Bay lighthouse.

Wategos Beach

A picturesque, sheltered cove ideal for swimming, stand-up paddleboarding, and longboarding. Known for its stunning scenery and relaxed atmosphere.

Little Wategos

The easternmost beach in Australia, offering a secluded and tranquil experience. Accessible via a short walk from Wategos Beach.

Tallow Beach

A long, expansive stretch of beach south of the lighthouse, popular for surfing, fishing, and peaceful walks away from the crowds.

Broken Head Beach

A beautiful, less crowded beach with excellent surfing conditions and scenic headland walks offering panoramic coastal views.

Brunswick Heads Beaches

Torakina Beach

A sheltered, picturesque beach at the mouth of the Brunswick River, perfect for families with young children due to its calm waters. A hidden gem just a short walk from Brunswick's charming cafes.

Main Beach (Brunswick Heads)

Offers long stretches of sand ideal for walking, swimming, and enjoying the relaxed coastal vibe of Brunswick Heads.

Other Notable Beaches

Belongil Beach

Stretching north from Byron's Main Beach, Belongil is known for its dog-friendly sections and relaxed atmosphere.

Kings Beach

A more secluded option, offering tranquility and natural beauty away from the busier beaches.

Lennox Head

A famous point break for surfers, with a stunning headland walk offering panoramic views of the coast.

Walks & National Parks

Goonengerry National Park

Just a 15-minute drive from Sunrise Estate, Goonengerry National Park is a hidden gem offering beautiful rainforest walks and stunning waterfalls.

Goonengerry Waterfalls

An easy walk to the waterfalls, which can be done as a loop. Enjoy the lush rainforest, lookouts, and opportunities for swimming in pristine pools.

South Boundary Loop

A 1.5 – 2 hour walk through shaded tracks, with a deviation to a beautiful waterfall. Perfect for a morning or afternoon adventure.

Minyon Falls (Nightcap National Park)

Approximately 20 minutes from Sunrise Estate, Minyon Falls is a spectacular natural wonder within Nightcap National Park, featuring one of Australia's most impressive waterfalls.

Minyon Falls Walking Track

Offers a range of forest experiences, pristine creeks, and breathtaking views of the waterfall. You can walk to the base of the falls for a refreshing swim.

Minyon Grass Trail

The quickest way to reach the base of Minyon Falls, just over 2km each way with less elevation gain. Perfect for a shorter adventure.

Other Notable Walks

Byron Bay Lighthouse Walk

An iconic walk offering panoramic views of the coastline, perfect for spotting dolphins and whales during migration season.

Rainforest Hikes

The entire Byron Hinterland is dotted with numerous trails through lush rainforests. Jarien, our qualified Nature Guide, can lead private, tailored hikes.

Exploration Tips

Best Time to Visit: Early mornings offer the best light for photography and fewer crowds on beaches and trails.

What to Bring: Sunscreen, water, comfortable walking shoes, and a light jacket for cooler mornings in the hinterland.

Guided Hikes: Jarien offers personalized nature hikes tailored to your fitness level and interests. Contact Paul to arrange.

Swimming: Always check local conditions before swimming. Some beaches have strong currents; ask locals for advice.

Wildlife: The region is home to diverse wildlife. Respect their space and observe from a distance.
