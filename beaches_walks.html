Beaches, Walks & Local Attractions

Waterfalls, beaches and wonders of the Byron Hinterland — most within a short drive of Sunrise Estate.

The Byron Hinterland is blessed with extraordinary natural beauty — from UNESCO rainforest and Australia's easternmost point to pristine beaches and hidden waterfalls. Here are our favourite places, grouped so you can plan an easy day out or a full-on adventure.

Right on Your Doorstep

These are the closest, most loved destinations near Sunrise Estate — all within 20 minutes.

Crystal Castle

Walking distance

One of Byron's best loved attractions — and practically next door. Crystal gardens, a Buddha walk, a rainforest labyrinth and thoughtful cafés. A 1-minute drive or a pleasant walk from the estate.

Goonengerry National Park

15 mins drive

A stunning local waterfall that's only a 10-minute walk from the car park. Shaded rainforest tracks, lookouts and swimmable pools. A genuine hidden gem.

Minyon Falls

20 mins drive

Spectacular waterfalls inside Nightcap National Park, with hikes for every ability — from an easy lookout to a full walk to the swimmable pool at the base.

Nightcap National Park

20–30 mins drive

UNESCO World Heritage Gondwana rainforest. Ancient trees, panoramic views and some of the most atmospheric walking in Australia.

Waterfalls & Rainforest Walks

Goonengerry National Park

Just 15 minutes from Sunrise Estate — beautiful rainforest walks and stunning waterfalls. The main waterfall is reachable in about a 10-minute walk from the car park.

Goonengerry Waterfalls

An easy walk to the waterfalls, often done as a loop. Lush rainforest, lookouts and pristine pools for swimming.

South Boundary Loop

A 1.5–2 hour walk through shaded tracks with a deviation to a beautiful waterfall. Perfect for a morning or afternoon adventure.

Minyon Falls (Nightcap National Park)

About 20 minutes from Sunrise Estate — one of Australia's most impressive waterfalls, inside UNESCO-listed Gondwana rainforest.

Minyon Falls Walking Track

Pristine creeks, forest and breathtaking views of the waterfall. You can walk to the base for a cool, refreshing swim.

Minyon Grass Trail

The quickest way to the base of Minyon Falls — just over 2 km each way with less elevation gain. Perfect for a shorter adventure.

Other Notable Walks

Cape Byron Lighthouse Walk

Iconic coastal walk with panoramic views — the easternmost point of mainland Australia. Ideal for spotting dolphins and, in season, whales.

Private Guided Rainforest Hikes

We can arrange private hikes with a qualified local Nature Guide — tailored to your pace, from gentle 30-minute waterfall walks to full-day forest adventures.

Beaches

Brunswick Heads

10–20 minutes away — the closest protected, family-friendly beaches.

Torakina Beach

Protected and calm — a sheltered, picturesque beach at the mouth of the Brunswick River. Perfect for families with young children and gentle swims. A hidden gem, just a short walk from Brunswick's cafés.

Main Beach (Brunswick Heads)

Long stretches of sand ideal for long walks, swimming and enjoying the relaxed coastal vibe of Brunswick Heads.

Byron Bay Beaches

20 minutes' drive.

Main Beach

The central hub of Byron Bay — perfect for swimming, sunbathing and people-watching. Easily accessible with plenty of facilities and a vibrant atmosphere.

Clarkes Beach

Popular with families and beginner surfers — calmer waters than Main Beach and beautiful views of the iconic Byron Bay lighthouse.

Wategos Beach

A picturesque, sheltered cove ideal for swimming, stand-up paddleboarding and longboarding. Stunning scenery and a relaxed atmosphere.

Little Wategos

The easternmost beach in Australia — secluded and tranquil, reached by a short walk from Wategos Beach.

Tallow Beach

A long, expansive stretch of beach south of the lighthouse — popular for surfing, fishing and peaceful walks away from the crowds.

Broken Head Beach

A beautiful, less crowded beach with excellent surfing and scenic headland walks with panoramic coastal views.

Other Notable Beaches

Belongil Beach

North from Byron's Main Beach — known for its dog-friendly sections and relaxed atmosphere.

Kings Beach

A more secluded option — tranquil and naturally beautiful, away from the busier beaches.

Lennox Head

A famous point break for surfers, with a stunning headland walk and panoramic ocean views.

Exploration Tips

Best time to visit: Early mornings offer the best light for photography and fewer crowds on beaches and trails.

What to bring: Sunscreen, water, comfortable walking shoes, and a light jacket for cooler hinterland mornings.

Guided hikes: We can arrange private nature hikes tailored to your fitness and interests — just ask when you enquire.

Swimming: Always check local conditions before swimming. Some beaches have strong currents; ask locals for advice.

Wildlife: The region is home to diverse wildlife. Respect their space and observe from a distance.
